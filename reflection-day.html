EVENT ORGANISER · STUDENT COUNCIL

Reflection Day Lead Organiser

Reflection Day is one of the most significant events in the IBDP calendar, a formal three-course dinner for 120 guests including students, parents, teachers, and senior leadership. As Lead Organiser, I built the entire event from scratch, coordinating over 30 students across every operational area while also directing, filming, and editing the event video shown on the night.

PHASE 1

Planning and Logistics

Planning began on 20 November with nothing in place. I built a payment collection system for student contributions, wrote a roles document assigning over 30 students to specific duties, and produced a full programme and timing plan. On top of that I handled menu planning, materials procurement, a hosting script, a seating plan, and a presentation, while also updating the event website. The scope was larger than anything I had organised before. Every element had to be coordinated simultaneously, and most of the decisions landed with me.

PHASE 2

Media Production

Alongside the logistics, I decided to direct, film, and edit the one-minute Reflection Day video to be screened to the full audience during the formal programme. Taking on both the operational and creative roles was a deliberate choice. Being inside the planning process gave me a clearer sense of what the event was trying to achieve, and I wanted the video to reflect that. I produced the full edit independently using Final Cut Pro.

PHASE 3

Executing on the Night

On the day, several students did not show up to their assigned roles, which required real-time reallocation to keep the programme running. The bigger challenge came during the video screening, when a full AV failure caused the audio to drop and the video to stall in front of the entire audience. I spent over an hour with the IT team diagnosing the problem, then improvised a live workaround by running the audio and video through separate systems simultaneously. The screening went ahead. Throughout this I was also co-hosting the formal programme, managing speech transitions, timing, and the three-course meal service. Everything ran together and the event was delivered in full.

PHASE 4

What I Took Away

The most useful thing I built that night was not on any document. Keeping operational pressure invisible to an audience of parents and senior leadership while solving real problems in real time is a different skill to planning. I also know what I would do differently. A full technical rehearsal on the actual school AV system days before would have prevented the failure entirely. Firmer confirmation systems for student roles would have reduced the no-shows. Both are simple lessons that cost real time on the day. I will carry them into anything I organise next.
